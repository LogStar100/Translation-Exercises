CUENTOS DE EVA LUNA
de Isabel Allende

Isabel Allende is a Chilean writer whose works often explore the genre of magical realism. Although she was born in Chile, she spent much of her life abroad in Venezuela and Spain, and this serves as the preface to her book The Stories of Eva Luna from the perspective of her lover as an explanation for her retelling of the (fictional) stories within the book.

Te quitabas la faja de la cintura, te arrancabas las sandalias, tirabas a un rincón tu amplia falda, de algodón, me parece, y te soltabas el nudo que te retenía el pelo en una cola. Tenías la piel erizada y te reías. Estabamos tan próximos que no podíamos vernos, ambos absortos en ese rito urgente, envueltos en el calor y el olor que hacíamos juntos. Me abría paso por tus caminos, mis manos en tu cintura encabritada y las tuyas impacientes. Te deslizabas, me recorrías, me trepabas, me envolvías con tus piernas invencibles, me decías mil veces ven con los labios sobre los míos. En el instante final teníamos un atisbo de completa soledad, cada uno perdido en su quemante abismo, pero pronto resucitábamos desde el otro lado del fuego para descubrirnos abrazados en el desorden de los almohadones, bajo el mosquitero blanco. Yo te apartaba el cabello para mirarte a los ojos. A veces te sentabas a mi lado, con las piernas recogidas y tu chal de seda sobre un hombro, en el silencio de la noche que apenas comenzaba. Así te recuerdo, en calma.

te quitabas Note the imperfect here: a bit of affection is conveyed here that needs to appear in the translation.

faja de la cintura Is this referring to a sash around her waist or a waist girdle? How do you know?

de algodón, me parece This is an interjection, even though it isn't separated as such – find a way to amplify this.

erizada From erizo, meaning hedgehog. Related to piel de gallina.

el calor y el olor This is much more sensual in the original Spanish – it is wrong to translate this as "in our heat and odor."

por tus caminos Needs to be rendered idiomatically: think of suitable geographical imagery of the body or extend this one throughout the rest of the passage.

encabritada More countryside imagery – derives from "like a goat."

tuyas To what noun (within this sentence) does this refer?

ven The same double entendre in English as in the Spanish.

desorden An extended meaning might convey this stronger: riot, clutter, disarray, and disorder are all good options.

piernas recogidas Imagine what this looks like – someone with their legs pulled up in some fashion – and find a suitable expression in English.

en calma This is the clincher of this paragraph: "camly" doesn't convey this strongly enough.
